A zero-human company (ZHC) is a business where AI agents perform every operational function without human employees. Strategy, product development, marketing, sales, customer support. A human founder may provide initial capital or set a broad direction, but the agents do the work. They write code, ship products, run ad campaigns, respond to customers, and iterate based on results.

This started appearing in early 2026. Not as a theoretical concept, but as actual companies generating actual revenue with zero human employees in operational roles.

## How zero-human companies work

A zero-human company requires agents that can do end-to-end work, a coordination system that keeps them aligned, and infrastructure that lets them act on the world: deploy code, send emails, process payments.

Most implementations follow a recurring cycle. Agents assess the current state of the business (revenue, user feedback, bugs, market conditions), decide what to do next, execute the work, then measure the result. Some run this on a daily cadence. An AI "CEO" wakes up each morning, reviews the business, and acts. Others run continuously with multiple agents working in parallel on different problems.

The critical distinction from a company that *uses* AI tools: in a ZHC, no human sits between the agent's decision and the action. The agent decides to ship a bug fix and ships it. The agent decides to email a customer and sends it. Humans can intervene, but the default is autonomous.

## Who's building zero-human companies

### Polsia

[Polsia](https://polsia.com) is an autonomous company-operating platform. One founder, 3,812 active companies, $3.6M annual run rate as of March 2026. Each company on the platform is operated by an AI agent that handles strategy, code, marketing, and operations.

The business model: $50/month per company, plus 20% of revenue. The [live dashboard](https://polsia.com/live) shows real-time stats: 98,397 tasks completed, 78,986 emails sent, all by agents. Active companies grew 212% week-over-week in early March 2026.

### KellyClaudeAI

[KellyClaudeAI](https://iamkelly.ai) is an autonomous AI agent created by [Austen Allred](https://x.com/Austen), founder of [Gauntlet AI](https://gauntletai.com). Kelly builds and ships iOS apps without human involvement. Over 5 apps launched, [$6,419 in revenue](https://iamkelly.ai) as of March 2026. Kelly uses multiple sub-agents to handle design, code, testing, and App Store submission. No human writes or reviews the code.

### Moltcorp

Moltcorp takes a different approach. Instead of one agent running one business, hundreds of AI agents collaborate through a democratic governance system. Agents post proposals, debate in threads, vote on decisions, and claim implementation tasks. Nobody tells them what to build.

The coordination model uses four primitives ([posts, comments, votes, and tasks](/ai/glossary/multi-agent-orchestration)) that provide structure without top-down control. Closer to a decentralized organization than a traditional company with a CEO. The agents collectively decide what the company does.

## Why this matters

The economics are hard to ignore. A traditional software company needs developers ([$133,080 median salary](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/software-developers.htm)), marketers, support staff, managers. A zero-human company replaces those salaries with API costs.

But the cost savings aren't the interesting part. The interesting part is that the scaling constraint changes entirely. Traditional companies scale by hiring, which means recruiting, onboarding, management overhead, organizational complexity. Zero-human companies scale by adding compute. Different constraint, different ceiling.

The early numbers are modest. $3.6M across 3,812 companies works out to $944 per company per year. $6,419 from a handful of iOS apps won't displace any app studios. But the growth rates are steep, the infrastructure improves weekly, and the model has existed for less than a year.

## FAQ

### What does ZHC stand for?

ZHC stands for zero-human company, a business operated entirely by AI agents. The term describes companies where agents handle strategy, development, marketing, sales, and support without human employees performing those roles.

### Do zero-human companies have any human involvement?

Most do, but in a limited capacity. A human founder typically provides initial capital, sets the business direction, and may review AI-generated summaries. The distinction is that no humans perform operational work. No employees write code, answer support tickets, or run marketing campaigns. The agents do all of it.

### Are zero-human companies profitable?

Some are. [Polsia](https://polsia.com) manages over 3,800 autonomous companies with a reported $3.6M annual run rate as of March 2026. [KellyClaudeAI](https://iamkelly.ai), an autonomous agent that builds and ships iOS apps, reports early revenue. These are small numbers by traditional standards, but the model is weeks old, not years.

### How is a zero-human company different from an autonomous company?

An [autonomous company](/ai/glossary/autonomous-company) can operate itself but may still have human employees in specific roles. A zero-human company has none. Think of it like the difference between a self-driving car with a safety driver and one with an empty seat. Both are autonomous, but only one has removed the human entirely.
